# Vice-President of India

> **Core Constitutional Idea**
> 
> _The Vice-President is a constitutional office designed to ensure continuity of the Republic while strengthening India's parliamentary and federal framework through his role as the ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha._

---

# 1. Identity

## Constitutional Position

- Second highest constitutional office in India.
- Constitutional office under **Article 63**.
- Ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.
- Acts as the President during vacancy or inability of the President.

---

## Constitutional Character

The Vice-President is **not part of the real executive**.

His ordinary constitutional role is parliamentary rather than executive.

The office combines two constitutional purposes:

- Continuity of the Head of State.
- Neutral presiding officer of the Council of States.

---

# 2. Constitutional Philosophy

The office reflects four constitutional principles:

## Constitutional Continuity

The Republic should never remain without a constitutional Head of State.

Hence, the Vice-President immediately discharges the functions of the President whenever necessary.

---

## Parliamentary Democracy

Instead of remaining an inactive standby office, the Vice-President performs a regular constitutional function by presiding over the Rajya Sabha.

This reflects the principle of **constitutional economy**, where one office performs two complementary constitutional roles.

---

## Federalism

As Chairman of the Rajya Sabha, the Vice-President presides over the House representing the States.

This symbolically reinforces the federal character of the Constitution despite its unitary bias.

---

## Institutional Neutrality

The Vice-President is not a member of either House.

Therefore, unlike ordinary members, he is expected to conduct the proceedings of the Rajya Sabha impartially and above day-to-day party politics.

# 4. Election

## Electoral College

The Vice-President is elected by:

- Elected Members of Lok Sabha
- Nominated Members of Lok Sabha
- Elected Members of Rajya Sabha
- Nominated Members of Rajya Sabha

**MLAs do not participate.**

---

## Method of Election

- Indirect Election
- Proportional Representation
- Single Transferable Vote
- Secret Ballot

---

## Why are MLAs excluded?

Unlike the President, the Vice-President does not ordinarily perform executive functions affecting the Union-State relationship.

His principal constitutional function is parliamentary.

Therefore, the electoral college consists only of Members of Parliament.

---

## Why are Nominated Members Included?

The Vice-President is the **ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha**.

Since nominated members fully participate in parliamentary proceedings, constitutional fairness requires that they also participate in electing their Presiding Officer.

This distinguishes the Vice-President's election from that of the President.

---

# 5. Qualifications

A person is qualified for election as Vice-President if he:

- Is a citizen of India.
- Has completed 35 years of age.
- Is qualified for election as a member of the Rajya Sabha.
- Does not hold any office of profit under the Union, State or local authority (subject to constitutional exceptions).

---

## Constitutional Logic

The Constitution prescribes minimum objective qualifications because the office derives legitimacy from constitutional purpose rather than political office.

The requirement of Rajya Sabha qualification reflects the Vice-President's primary parliamentary role as Chairman of the Council of States.


# 6. Oath

## Constitutional Basis

**Article 69**

Before entering office, the Vice-President takes an oath or affirmation before the President (or a person appointed by the President).

---

## Oath

The Vice-President swears to:

- Bear true faith and allegiance to the Constitution of India.
- Faithfully discharge the duties of the office.

---

## Constitutional Philosophy

Unlike the President, whose oath includes the duty to **preserve, protect and defend the Constitution**, the Vice-President's oath reflects his primary role as a constitutional functionary and presiding officer.

The oath signifies allegiance to the Constitution rather than to any political authority.

---

# 7. Term of Office

## Constitutional Basis

**Article 67**

- Term: **Five years**
- Eligible for re-election.
- Continues in office until the successor enters office, ensuring constitutional continuity.

---

## Resignation

The Vice-President may resign by addressing his resignation to the **President**.

---

# 8. Vacancy

A vacancy may arise due to:

- Expiry of term.
- Resignation.
- Removal.
- Death.
- Otherwise becoming disqualified.

---

## Constitutional Safeguard

A vacancy must be filled **as soon as possible**.

If the vacancy occurs due to expiry of the normal term, the election should be completed before the expiry of the term.

If it occurs due to death, resignation or removal, the election should be held **as soon as possible**, and the elected Vice-President serves a fresh five-year term from the date of assuming office.

---

## Constitutional Philosophy

The office is never intended to remain vacant for long because it is central to:

- Continuity of the Presidency.
- Smooth functioning of the Rajya Sabha.

---

# 9. Removal

## Constitutional Basis

**Article 67(b)**

The Vice-President may be removed by:

- A resolution of the **Rajya Sabha** passed by an **effective majority** (majority of all the then members of the House), and
- Agreed to by the **Lok Sabha** by a simple majority.

A **14-day notice** is mandatory before moving the resolution.

---

## Grounds for Removal

The Constitution **does not prescribe any specific grounds**.

---

## Constitutional Philosophy

Unlike the President, the Vice-President is **not impeached** because his ordinary constitutional role is that of the **Chairman of the Rajya Sabha**, not the Head of State.

A presiding officer must enjoy the confidence of the House whose proceedings he conducts.

Therefore:

- **Rajya Sabha initiates** the removal as it is directly affected by his functioning.
- **Lok Sabha concurs** because the Vice-President is a constitutional office of the Union.

This reflects the principle that **the removal mechanism follows the primary constitutional function of the office**.

---

# 10. Powers and Functions

## A. Ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha (Article 64)

The Vice-President:

- Presides over the sittings of the Rajya Sabha.
- Maintains order and decorum.
- Interprets and applies the Rules of Procedure.
- Decides who may speak.
- Protects the rights and privileges of members.
- Exercises a **casting vote** in case of a tie; he has **no ordinary vote**.

---

## Constitutional Philosophy

As the presiding officer of the Council of States, the Vice-President safeguards:

- Parliamentary deliberation.
- Institutional neutrality.
- Federal representation.

---

## B. Acting as President (Article 65)

The Vice-President acts as the President when:

- The office of the President falls vacant due to death, resignation, removal or otherwise.
- The President is unable to discharge the functions of the office due to absence, illness or any other cause.

---

## Important Distinction

The Vice-President **does not become the President**.

He merely **acts as** or **discharges the functions of** the President until a new President assumes office.

---

## Why?

The Vice-President is elected by **Members of Parliament only**, whereas the President is elected by an Electoral College comprising **MPs and MLAs**.

Therefore, the Vice-President does not possess the same democratic and federal mandate required to permanently occupy the office of the President.

He ensures **continuity of the office**, while a fresh election restores **constitutional legitimacy**.

---

## During this Period

While acting as President:

- He ceases to perform the duties of the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.
- He exercises all the powers, functions and immunities of the President.

---

# 11. Judicial Interpretation

## Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's Constituent Assembly Vision

The Vice-President was conceived as an office ensuring:

- Continuity of the Presidency.
- Efficient utilisation of a high constitutional office through the Chairmanship of the Rajya Sabha.

---

## Constitutional Position

No major Supreme Court judgment has fundamentally altered the constitutional position of the Vice-President.

The office is interpreted primarily through:

- Parliamentary conventions.
- Constitutional practice.
- The text of Articles 63–71.

# 15. LEA Reflection _(Not for Answer Writing)_

## Architectural Insight

The Constitution does not create the office of the Vice-President merely as a substitute for the President. Instead, it combines **two complementary constitutional functions** into a single office:

1. **Constitutional Continuity** — ensuring the Republic is never without a Head of State.
2. **Institutional Neutrality** — providing an impartial presiding officer for the Rajya Sabha, the federal chamber of Parliament.

The differing methods of election and removal demonstrate a recurring constitutional design principle:

> **The constitutional design of an office—its election, powers and removal—flows from its primary constitutional purpose rather than its formal rank.**

Thus, the Vice-President safeguards both the **continuity of the Republic** and the **dignity of India's bicameral federal Parliament**.
