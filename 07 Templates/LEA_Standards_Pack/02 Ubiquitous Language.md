# LEA Ubiquitous Language

## Purpose

The Ubiquitous Language defines the canonical vocabulary of the Learning Enterprise Architecture (LEA).

Every architectural term shall have **one and only one meaning** across the entire enterprise, including:

- Architecture
- Learning
- Knowledge
- Assessment
- Governance
- ChatGPT Projects
- Obsidian Repository
- Documentation

Its purpose is to eliminate ambiguity, establish a shared understanding, and ensure every Engine communicates using a consistent domain language.

The Ubiquitous Language serves as the semantic foundation of LEA.

---

# Principles

- One Concept → One Term
- One Term → One Definition
- Every definition is canonical.
- Synonyms are discouraged.
- New architectural concepts must be added here before adoption.
- Changes require an Architecture Review.

---

# Enterprise

## Learning Enterprise

The complete socio-technical system responsible for acquiring, organizing, assessing, governing, and continuously improving knowledge.

---

## Engine

A bounded business domain responsible for managing one Primary Stock.

Each Engine owns its own purpose, capabilities, information model, artifacts, and lifecycle.

---

## Capability

A stable business ability owned by an Engine.

Capabilities define **what** an Engine must be able to do, independent of implementation.

---

## Process

A sequence of activities that realizes a Capability.

Processes describe **how** a Capability is executed.

---

## Workflow

A concrete implementation of a Process.

Workflows may vary without changing the underlying Capability.

---

## Primary Stock

The primary enterprise asset stewarded by an Engine.

Examples:

- Learner Competence
- Canonical Knowledge
- Assessment Evidence
- Enterprise Intelligence

---

# Knowledge Domain

## Theme

The atomic knowledge artifact of LEA.

A Theme owns one coherent body of knowledge and serves as the canonical source of truth for that domain.

---

## Composite Theme

A Theme that groups multiple Core Themes into a coherent learning domain.

Composite Themes orchestrate knowledge but do not own detailed knowledge.

---

## Theme Map

A conceptual relationship model connecting Themes.

Theme Maps contain Theme nodes only and never duplicate Theme content.

---

## Knowledge Asset

Any canonical artifact owned by the Knowledge Engine.

Examples include Themes, Composite Themes, Theme Maps, and supporting metadata.

---

# Learning Domain

## Learning Session

A bounded period of intentional learning focused on improving learner competence.

---

## Understanding

The stage where concepts are comprehended before documentation.

---

## Internalization

The stage where knowledge becomes mentally organized and retrievable.

---

## Integration

Connecting newly acquired knowledge with existing Themes.

---

## Reinforcement

Activities that strengthen long-term retention.

---

## Application

Using knowledge to solve problems, answer questions, and reason effectively.

---

## Reflection

Evaluating learning outcomes to identify improvements and guide future learning.

---

# Assessment Domain

## Assessment

The process of objectively measuring learner competence.

---

## Assessment Evidence

The accumulated body of evidence describing learner performance and competency.

---

## Competency

A measurable demonstration of understanding and application.

---

## Feedback

Actionable information generated by the Assessment Engine to improve learning.

---

# Governance Domain

## Enterprise Intelligence

The accumulated strategic knowledge used to optimize and evolve the Learning Enterprise.

---

## Standard

A mandatory enterprise rule governing architecture, learning, knowledge, or implementation.

---

## ADR (Architecture Decision Record)

A permanent record of a significant architectural decision affecting LEA.

---

## Design Journal

A chronological record documenting the evolution of LEA and the rationale behind architectural changes.

---

## Output Contract

The specification defining how LEA artifacts are produced and presented.

---

# Repository

## Obsidian

The canonical Knowledge Repository of LEA.

All published Themes and Theme Maps reside in Obsidian.

---

## ChatGPT Project

A bounded conversational context implementing the Learning Engine.

ChatGPT acts as the **Dialectic Learning Partner**, facilitating understanding and reasoning, but it is not the Knowledge Repository.

---

# Reserved Terms

The following concepts are reserved for future architectural evolution and shall not be used until formally defined.

- Knowledge Object
- Learning Object
- Competency Map
- Question Envelope
- Knowledge Graph

---

# Architecture Principle

> **If a concept cannot be defined unambiguously within the Ubiquitous Language, it is not yet part of the LEA Architecture.**
