

> *The Islands of India are geological extensions of the Indian Plate into the surrounding seas. While the Andaman & Nicobar Islands originated through active plate tectonics and volcanism, the Lakshadweep Islands are products of coral growth over submerged volcanic structures. Together, they represent two fundamentally different mechanisms of island formation.*

---

# Theme Essence

India possesses two distinct island groups with completely different geological origins.

- **Andaman & Nicobar Islands** → Tectonic and Volcanic Origin
- **Lakshadweep Islands** → Coral (Biogenic) Origin

Understanding their genesis explains their relief, ecology, strategic importance and environmental vulnerabilities.

# 2. Lakshadweep Islands

## Geological Origin

The Lakshadweep Islands are **coral atolls** that developed over submerged volcanic mountains formed during the passage of the Indian Plate over the **Réunion Hotspot**.

The volcanic islands later became extinct and gradually subsided beneath sea level.

Coral polyps colonized these submerged volcanic platforms and continued growing upward, eventually forming coral islands.

---

## Darwin's Subsidence Theory

The volcanic island gradually subsides due to cooling and tectonic adjustment.

As long as corals remain within the sunlit zone, they continue growing upward at nearly the same rate.

Eventually,

- the volcanic island sinks,
- coral reefs remain near sea level,
- a central lagoon develops,

forming a **coral atoll**.

# 3. Andaman & Nicobar Islands

## Geological Origin

The Andaman & Nicobar Islands are part of an **active volcanic island arc** formed due to subduction.

The Indian Plate is continuously subducting beneath the Burma (Myanmar) Microplate.

Partial melting generates magma,

which rises through the crust,

forming volcanic islands.

---

## Characteristics

- Mountainous islands.
- Active tectonic region.
- Frequent earthquakes.
- Deep oceanic trench nearby.
- Dense tropical forests.
- Rich marine ecosystems.

---

## Barren Island

- India's only active volcano.
- Continues to receive magma from the active subduction zone.
- Demonstrates ongoing plate tectonic activity.

---

## Narcondam Island

- Extinct volcanic island.
- Product of earlier volcanic activity.

---

# Why is Barren Island Still Active?

Unlike Lakshadweep,

the Andaman region lies directly above an active subduction zone.

Therefore,

subduction continues,

fresh magma is continuously generated,

resulting in active volcanism.

In contrast,

Lakshadweep moved away from the Réunion Hotspot millions of years ago,

cutting off its magma supply.

# 5. Strategic Importance

## Andaman & Nicobar Islands

### Maritime Location

Located close to the **Strait of Malacca**,

one of the world's busiest maritime choke points.

---

### Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ)

The islands significantly expand India's EEZ,

providing greater control over:

- Marine resources.
- Fisheries.
- Offshore energy.
- Seabed minerals.

---

### Naval Importance

The islands serve as a forward operating base for:

- Indian Navy.
- Indian Air Force.
- Maritime surveillance.
- Disaster response.

---

### Indo-Pacific Importance

Their strategic location enables India to monitor major Sea Lines of Communication (SLOCs),

strengthening India's role in the Indo-Pacific.
