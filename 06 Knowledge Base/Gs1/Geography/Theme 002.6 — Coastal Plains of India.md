# Theme 002.6 — Coastal Plains of India

> *The Coastal Plains of India represent the interface between the Peninsular Plateau and the surrounding seas. Their present physiography is governed by the interaction of tectonic evolution, river systems, marine processes and sediment deposition, resulting in two contrasting coastal landscapes.*

---

# Theme Essence

The Coastal Plains developed along the western and eastern margins of Peninsular India after the breakup of Gondwanaland. Although both border the sea, they differ fundamentally because of differences in tectonic evolution, plateau slope, river length, sediment load and marine processes.

---

# 1. Genesis of the Coastal Plains

A coastal plain develops where **river deposition exceeds marine erosion** over a continental shelf.

# 2. Why are the Western and Eastern Coastal Plains Different?

The contrasting nature of India's coastal plains originates from the geological evolution of the Peninsular Plateau.

---

## Western Coastal Plain

### Geological Basis

During the breakup of Gondwanaland,

the western continental margin underwent faulting and subsidence.

This produced:

- Western Ghats close to the coastline.
- Steep westward gradient.
- Narrow continental margin.

---

### Consequences

- Short, swift rivers.
- Limited sediment deposition.
- Narrow coastal plain.
- Predominance of estuaries.

---

### Systems Chain

Western Margin Faulting

↓

Western Ghats close to Coast

↓

Steep Gradient

↓

Short Rivers

↓

Limited Sediment Deposition

↓

Narrow Coastal Plain

↓

Estuaries

---

## Eastern Coastal Plain

### Geological Basis

The Peninsular Plateau acquired a general eastward slope.

Consequently,

major rivers traverse long distances before entering the Bay of Bengal.

---

### Consequences

- Long rivers.
- Large drainage basins.
- High sediment load.
- Broad continental shelf.
- Extensive delta formation.
- Broad coastal plain.

---

# 3. Estuaries vs Deltas

## Why are estuaries common on the Western Coast?

Reasons

- Short high-energy rivers.
- Limited sediment deposition.
- Submerged coastline.
- Strong marine action.

Result

↓

Estuarine river mouths.

Examples

- Narmada
- Tapi
- Mandovi

---

## Why are deltas common on the Eastern Coast?

Reasons

- Long rivers.
- Large catchment areas.
- Huge sediment load.
- Gentle coastal slope.
- Broad continental shelf.

Result

↓

Delta formation.

Examples

- Mahanadi
- Godavari
- Krishna
- Kaveri

# 4. Western Coastal Plain

## Konkan Coast

### Location

Between Gujarat and Goa.

### Characteristics

- Narrow coastal plain.
- Numerous estuaries.
- Rocky coastline.
- Important natural harbours.

---

## Kanara Coast

### Location

Karnataka Coast.

### Characteristics

- Narrow coastal strip.
- Heavy Southwest Monsoon rainfall.
- Dense forests.
- Short west-flowing rivers.

---

## Malabar Coast

### Location

Kerala Coast.

### Characteristics

- Narrow coastal plain.
- Backwaters (Kayals).
- Coconut and spice cultivation.
- Rich fisheries.
- Dense settlement.

---

## Why are Backwaters formed on the Malabar Coast?

Short rivers deposit sediments near the coast.

Longshore currents redistribute these sediments,

forming barrier beaches and sand bars.

Water becomes enclosed behind these barriers,

forming lagoons or backwaters.

### Systems Chain

Short Rivers

↓

Nearshore Sediment Deposition

↓

Longshore Currents

↓

Barrier Beaches

↓

Enclosed Water Bodies

↓

Backwaters

Examples

- Vembanad Lake
- Ashtamudi Lake

---

# 5. Eastern Coastal Plain

## Utkal Coast

### Location

Odisha Coast.

### Characteristics

- Broad coastal plain.
- Dominated by the Mahanadi Delta.
- Fertile alluvial deposits.
- Presence of Chilika Lake.
- Cyclone-prone coastline.

---

## Andhra Coast

### Location

Andhra Pradesh Coast.

### Characteristics

- Broad deltaic plain.
- Formed by the Godavari and Krishna rivers.
- Highly fertile.
- Important agricultural region.
- Major ports and fishing centres.

---

## Coromandel Coast

### Location

Tamil Nadu Coast.

### Characteristics

- Dominated by the Kaveri Delta.
- Comparatively straighter coastline.
- Receives significant rainfall from the Northeast Monsoon.
- Important agricultural and urban region.

## Core Identity

> **The Coastal Plains of India are products of the interaction between tectonic evolution, river systems and marine processes. The Western Coastal Plain is a narrow, estuarine coast shaped by faulting and short rivers, whereas the Eastern Coastal Plain is a broad, deltaic coast built by long rivers depositing vast quantities of sediments.**
