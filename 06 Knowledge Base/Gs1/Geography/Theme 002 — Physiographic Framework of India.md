# Theme Identity

## Definition

The **Physiographic Framework of India** is the integrated physical structure of the Indian subcontinent produced through billions of years of geological evolution, plate tectonics, volcanism, denudation, fluvial deposition, aeolian processes and marine action.

Rather than representing isolated landforms, India's physiographic divisions form an interconnected geological system that controls the country's climate, drainage, soils, biodiversity, agriculture, settlements, economy and strategic geography.

---

# Theme Purpose

This theme establishes the **physical foundation of India**.

Every subsequent theme in Indian Geography—including drainage, monsoon, soils, vegetation, agriculture, disasters and human geography—derives from this framework.

It therefore serves as the **gateway theme** for understanding Indian Geography as an integrated system rather than a collection of independent chapters.
